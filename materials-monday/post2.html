Have you heard about Proteomics? If yes, then this post will add more to your knowledge, and if not, then this is the post for you to get familiar with it.

So what is Proteomics? .... The study of proteomes on a large scale is called Proteomics. This proteome is an expression of an organism’s complete set of proteins. The word ‘proteome’ can be used in contexts like the proteomes of species (Homosapiens) or proteomes of organs (heart). The proteomes are not constant; they change from cell to cell and also over a time span. Nanoparticles are playing an important role in the study of proteomes. These nanoparticles are used to study the different functions and activities of proteomes. As we know that various activities of living beings are controlled and coordinated with the association of proteins, the study of proteomics can be helpful in curing lethal diseases. Many properties of Nanomaterials greatly influence their interactions with biomolecules and cells, some of which are listed below: Size, shape, chemical composition, surface, structure, charge, aggregation, agglomeration, solubility. Hence, proteomics has a close connection with materials science, and there is a lot of scope for research and development for Materials Scientists in this field.
