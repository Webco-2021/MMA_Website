An optical fiber is a flexible, transparent fiber made by drawing glass (silica) or plastic to a diameter slightly thicker than that of a human hair. .... Optical fibers like this are used as a medium to transmit light from one end to the other. They can be used for sending data down communications cables with higher bandwidth (faster data rate) over long distances and have benefits such as less data loss and interference compared to electrical cables. Standard optical fibers are made by first constructing a large-diameter "preform" with a carefully controlled refractive index profile and then "pulling" the preform to form the long, thin optical fiber. The preform is commonly made by three chemical vapor deposition methods: inside vapor deposition, outside vapor deposition, and vapor axial deposition. Materials Scientists have contributed a lot to the field of manufacturing optical fibers. They have to be manufactured cautiously, especially the difference in the refractive index of core and cladding have to be maintained appropriately for transmission to be fast with less attenuation. But there are still some practical issues associated with optical fibers like Termination and splicing, Free-space coupling, Fiber fuse, and most importantly, Chromatic dispersion. There is scope for materials scientists to contribute significantly to the issue of Chromatic dispersion by suggesting solutions for the changes required in materials properties.
